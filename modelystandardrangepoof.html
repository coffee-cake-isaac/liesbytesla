Elon Musk stated in July of 2020, that the standard range variant would have been "unacceptably low". Let's unpack this a little bit.

  1. If we look at the above image, taken from the Tesla Model Y reveal video here, Tesla has the standard range listed as $39,000... But one of the important things here is that the image says 230 miles. That means, Tesla was entirely aware of this in March 2019. In July of 2020, he stated that the range of the standard range Model Y is "unacceptable". If being less than 250 miles makes it "unacceptably low", then why even put it on the list of models to begin with? What a shitty thing to do... mislead consumers.

    BUT WAIT -- THERES MORE Does anyone happen to know the range of the Standard Range Plus Model 3 when it launched and until Oct. 2019 according to this article from Electrek. THAT'S RIGHT! Less than 250 miles! It was at 240 miles. But then, the range suddenly went up 10 miles without any explanation from anyone? Hardware? Software? Was it the introduction of full-one-pedal driving? Who the fuck knows because Tesla never said why.

    BUT WAIT -- THERES EVEN MORE Does anyone happen to know the range of the off-menu Model 3 Standard Range? RIGHT AGAIN. LESS THAN 250 MILES. That variant will net you about 220 miles. So, if "250 miles is unacceptable", why do they currently sell the Standard Range Model 3, and why did they sell the Standard Range Plus variant until Oct 2019?
    Speculation: There's not enough meat on the bones for Tesla to warrant selling the Standard Range Model Y. Which, I mean, Tesla's a business. They're not a charity. If the profit margin is too thin, just be transparent. Don't pretend like you're doing it because it "doesn't meet your standards". We're adults. We can handle some bad news every once and a while.
